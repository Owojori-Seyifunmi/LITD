LITD CIC delivers safeguarding-led support that helps vulnerable young people and displaced adults rebuild stability, develop independence, and integrate positively into their communities.

Our Services

Supporting the Disadvantaged With Care, Dignity, and Purpose

As a Community Interest Company, LITD C.I.C. delivers holistic, person-centred support to individuals, families, and young people facing social, emotional, and economic challenges.

Mental Health Support and Awareness

We provide accessible mental health and wellbeing support through trusted professionals and community-based initiatives.

Free services

What we offer:

  • Partnerships with psychologists and mental health professionals
  • Counselling, coaching, and therapy tailored to individual needs
  • Mental health workshops and awareness programmes
  • Peer support and wellbeing sessions
  • Advocacy to reduce the risk of self-harm
  • Support with understanding and managing PTSD

Additional Support

Supporting Vulnerable Communities

The majority of our service users come from Black, Asian, and Ethnic Minority (BAME) communities, though our support is not limited to these groups. Many are failed asylum seekers who have not been granted leave to remain, leaving them in a state of extreme vulnerability.

Wellbeing Sessions & Peer Support

Regular wellbeing sessions and peer support groups for mental and emotional health.

Welfare Support & Signposting

Guidance and referrals to appropriate services and support networks.

Community Integration

Supporting transition, integration, and development within UK society.

Social Events & Workshops

Organization of social events, conferences, and workshops for community engagement.

Youth Focus

Addressing the Frustrations of Young People

We have observed a growing sense of anger and frustration among young people in BAME communities, particularly those from vulnerable families. Many witness their parents struggling under financial hardship, systemic barriers, and discrimination, which can leave them feeling disillusioned and disconnected.

If left unaddressed, these challenges risk fostering disengagement, social isolation, and even destructive behaviors.

At LITD, we are committed to breaking this cycle.

We actively engage young people in positive opportunities that channel their energy into personal growth, skill development, and community participation. Our goal is to empower them with the tools, confidence, and resilience needed to overcome adversity and build a brighter future.

Our Solutions

Practical Support That Makes a Difference

Food Bank

Many service users run out of funds to pay for food before they receive their next benefits. LITD CIC supports service users by providing a food bank, including culturally appropriate food items.

Financial Support

We support service users to visit local poverty charities such as Gateway M40 Debt & Advice, Cheetham Hill Advice center and North Manchester Community Partnership. These charities can provide in-depth support regarding benefits and extra financial support.

Empowering

Equip disadvantaged individuals and their families with life empowering tools to create a better life and reduce their dependency on governmental agencies.

Encouraging Participation

Beyond providing direct support, we foster active participation. Our beneficiaries are given the opportunity to engage in decision-making processes regarding the running and future initiatives of LITD.
