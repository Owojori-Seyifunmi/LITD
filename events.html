Messages of Support

Words of encouragement from special guests who couldn't be with us in person at International Women's Day 2025

Angela Rayner MP

Ashton-under-Lyne, Audenshaw, Droylsden and Dukinfield

"I'm sorry I can't be with you in person today at this inspirational event and I hope you all have an amazing time.

For me, International Women's Day is an opportunity to highlight the accomplishments and achievements of women everywhere and also to offer our support and solidarity to those that are struggling for rights across the globe.

I am incredibly lucky to know and to work with some phenomenal women who continue to nourish and to inspire me in so many ways.

My message to women everywhere is – there is only one of you and that is your power. The only person that gets to decide what you can achieve is YOU."

— Sent via Rosina Oliver-Lawlor, Office Manager

Sharman Birtles, MBE JP DL

Vice Lord-Lieutenant of Greater Manchester

"I'm so sorry not to be with you today as you celebrate International Women's Day in Tameside, but I wanted to send you my very best wishes for a successful, interesting and enjoyable event.

Women across the World have always faced many issues which continue to this day. That's why this year's theme 'Accelerate Action' is so important. We have been patient for so long to see gender equality and we must now increase momentum in addressing the barriers which we face.

This is not an issue we can leave to others.
WE must take decisive steps in supporting one another, particularly those who face a range of challenges.
WE must always support one another and show resilience and courage.
WE are strong.
WE are wonderful.
WE can do this!"

Cllr Betty Affleck

Civic Mayor of Tameside

"I'm so sorry I'm unable to be with you today for the International Women's Day event at Holy Trinity Church and Community Centre. It's a place where so much good work is done in the west end of Ashton and beyond.

I'm sure it's going to be a great day as you have such an exciting programme to look forward to. There's lots of food, fun and fashion, and some ladies are going to get some very well-deserved pampering. I wish I could enjoy some of it myself.

However, even though I can't be with you, I want you to be certain that you have my full support.

I have always been very proud of the role women play in society and of their many achievements. I never cease to admire their strength and resilience. I gained my inspiration for that from my mother, Rose, who not only raised a large family but was able to support my father, Joe, as a Tameside councillor, and to serve as deputy mayoress and mayoress of this borough.

So please be assured of my very best wishes. I wish Light in the Darkness every success with the very valuable work they do."

Angela Robbins

Director, Well Polished Cleaning Agency

"I wish you many blessings and love at your International Women's Day event today.

I am very proud to sponsor this event through my company Well Polished Cleaning Agency.

As Past President of Tameside Rotary, I know, that by giving a little of yourself, you get SO much love and happiness back. Working as a team and learning and growing in confidence with the help of your colleagues. I know this will be happening amongst the volunteers at LITD.

I did explain to Beatrice when I met her that operating where she was living in Manchester meant that she would be better placed to approach a Manchester Rotary Club for funding. Beatrice looked at me with great determination and sincerity and said, that as she felt so much support in our Tameside community, she WOULD have an event in Tameside and I absolutely knew she would achieve it one way or another!

Beatrice, you inspire me. I see the determination, I hear what you've been through and the love and hope generated from you will spread to everyone attending your event today.

Well done to Beatrice and all who volunteer at Light in the Darkness."
