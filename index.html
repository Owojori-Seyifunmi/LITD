About Us

Light In The Darkness (LITD C.I.C.)

Light In The Darkness is a Community Interest Company based in Greater Manchester, dedicated to bringing hope, practical support, and advocacy to society's most vulnerable individuals.

We work with marginalised families and individuals who struggle to access statutory services. Through personalised, compassionate interventions, we empower people facing hardship to regain stability, confidence, and independence.

Person-Centred and Strength-Based Approach

We identify individuals and families who are not receiving the support they need and provide care tailored to their unique circumstances.

Why Our Work Matters

Bridging the Gap When Systems Fail

In an ideal system, statutory services would support individuals before they reach crisis point. In reality, many only receive help when they are already in severe distress.

Since 2017, we have witnessed systemic bias and institutional barriers within healthcare, education, social services, and the justice system. These failures disproportionately affect vulnerable communities and often go unchallenged.

LITD exists to bridge this gap, advocate for fairness, and ensure dignity is upheld.

When accountability is resisted, advocacy becomes essential. We stand alongside our beneficiaries to ensure they are treated fairly.

The Reality on the Ground

We regularly support individuals facing:

Children unable to attend school due to unresolved safeguarding issues

Parents and children experiencing suicidal thoughts due to neglect

Families living in unsafe housing conditions

Young people experiencing hate crimes without adequate intervention

Individuals trapped in mental health crises within rigid systems

Our Services

Supporting the Disadvantaged With Care, Dignity, and Purpose

As a Community Interest Company, LITD C.I.C. delivers holistic, person-centred support to individuals, families, and young people facing social, emotional, and economic challenges.

Mental Health Support

Accessible mental health and wellbeing support through trusted professionals and community-based initiatives.

  • Partnerships with psychologists
  • Counselling and therapy
  • Mental health workshops
  • PTSD support

Youth Empowerment

We work with young people to prevent disengagement, build confidence, and create positive pathways for the future.

  • Digital and sports initiatives
  • 6-month entrepreneurship programme
  • Volunteering opportunities
  • Leadership skills

Educational Opportunities

Supporting individuals, especially women from marginalised communities, to access education and achieve independence.

  • Recognised qualifications
  • Leadership training
  • Hair wrapping sessions
  • CIC start-up support

Social Prescribing

Non-clinical support that addresses the root causes of poor wellbeing and social exclusion.

  • Grief support
  • Domestic violence pathways
  • Walking groups
  • Befriending programmes

Culturally Appropriate Food Bank

Addressing a critical gap in traditional food aid services by ensuring dignity and cultural familiarity.

  • Food support during benefits wait
  • Culturally appropriate items
  • African chef collaboration
  • Nutritional suitability

Our Support Model

The Need

We primarily support individuals from Global Majority communities affected by trauma, systemic oppression, poverty, and poor mental or physical health. Rather than defining people by their struggles, we focus on their strengths, skills, and potential.

Our Purpose

We help individuals reconnect with their abilities, rebuild confidence and self-worth, navigate ongoing barriers such as poverty, racism, and health challenges, and move forward with dignity and hope.
